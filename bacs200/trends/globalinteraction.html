Trends That Are Shaping Our Future

What our tomorrow may look like

Global Interaction

Within the past few decades, we have already seen exponential change in how we interact with one another globally in almost all facets of life. Business, recreation, friendships and other relationships, and communication in general have all been deeply impacted by internet and travel technology. This is a trend that is continuing, causing the world to become more and more accessible to everyone, everywhere. Businesses of all sizes, friends, and everyone in between are able to conduct meetings, send messages, and find data in seconds because of our current electronic infrastructure. Moreover, because of the continued interest in refining virtual reality technology, the possibilities for global interactions are being blown open. It is becoming increasingly plausible for people to be within reach of one another digitally while being completely physically separated.

Furthermore, global interactions are being considered under the current conditions and effects of the COVID-19 pandemic. Are people going to be as inclined to physically visit one another? Are countries going to allow foreigners into their borders as easily as before? Is the world going to continue to become more open, or more restricted? Expert answers to these questions are actually somewhat inconclusive; there are qualified people arguing both sides. Albeit the majority of available information indicates that cultures around the world are going to become more selective on who has access to their countries. This was exacerbated by the pandemic, in which genuine trust for outside individuals dropped immensely. Moreover, with increased technological innovations, in-person interactions are becoming less sought after.
