Education

The system of education has been somewhat stagnant in the ideals that it wishes to establish within those developing within society. Across the globe, STEM subjects are prioritized in industrialized nations while developing countries focus on teaching necessary duties and responsibilities that are needed to survive and upkeep social operations. Albeit, in developed countries, the forms through which education is delivered and the priorities of the curriculum are shifting. As schools had to adapt to online formats for lessons, the world has been seeing an ever-increasing adjustment towards virtual learning opportunities. While classrooms have long experimented with utilizing technology to further the capabilities of lesson plans, the impact of COVID-19 has caused a large gravitation towards online and electronic learning.

Moreover, personalized learning environments for students have been gaining popularity. Called micro-schools or micro-pods, these classrooms focus on individual student needs. This trend allows for a more personal educational experience tailored to specific strengths and areas for improvement for students. Going hand in hand with this focus on education comes a desire to focus on cultivating creativity in the classroom. As professions shift from cut and dry processes to needs to think outside the box to solve complex problems, educators deem it essential to prepare students for how to think creatively. Furthermore, because of the place that expression and identity play into our daily lives as people, giving developing youths tools to find themselves in various ways has become a priority. Finally, educators are also trying to find more interactive means for students to learn.
